Joan of Arc

A French peasant girl, led French troops to victory against the English during the Hundred Years' War

Here's a time line of Joan of Arc's life:

  • 1412: Born in Domrémy, France.
  • 1425: Began hearing voices, believed to be from saints Michael, Catherine, and Margaret, urging her to support Charles VII and liberate France.
  • 1428 (May): Traveled to Vaucouleurs to seek support from Robert de Baudricourt to meet Charles VII; initially dismissed.
  • 1429 (January): Made a second journey to Vaucouleurs, gaining Baudricourt's support and setting out to meet Charles VII.
  • 1429 (March): Met Charles VII in Chinon and convinced him of her divine mission.
  • 1429 (April 29): Arrived in Orléans to relieve the siege.
  • 1429 (May 8): Led French troops to a decisive victory at Orléans, lifting the siege.
  • 1429 (June 18): Led French forces to victory at the Battle of Patay
  • 1429 (July 17): Present at the coronation of Charles VII in Reims, fulfilling one of her divine missions.
  • 1430 (May 23): Captured by Burgundian forces during the defense of Compiègne.
  • 1430 (November): Sold to the English by the Burgundians.
  • 1431 (January 9): Trial for heresy began in Rouen, orchestrated by the English.
  • 1431 (May 30): Executed by burning at the stake in Rouen at the age of 19.
  • 1456: Posthumously exonerated in a retrial ordered by Pope Callixtus III, declaring her innocence and martyrdom.
  • 1920: Canonized as a saint by Pope Benedict XV.
“I studied that girl, Joan of Arc, for twelve years and it never seemed to me that the artists and the writers gave us a true picture of her. They drew a picture of a peasant. But they always missed the face - the divine soul, the pure character, the supreme woman, the wonderful girl.”

-- Mark Twain
